## 🧠 Deep Macro Theme

**The Kashkari-Iran Double Bind**

The convergence of Kashkari's rate hike signal and the Iran military escalation creates a policy trap with no clean exit path. If the Fed hikes to combat inflation (as Kashkari suggests), it tightens financial conditions into a geopolitical shock — amplifying the recession risk from trade disruption and energy uncertainty. If the Fed cuts to buffer the geopolitical shock, it validates the inflation that Iran-driven supply risks could exacerbate. This is the classic "supply shock + demand shock" policy dilemma that broke central banks in the 1970s. The market is currently pricing the "Fed blinks and cuts" scenario (2Y rallying) — but Kashkari's comments suggest the Fed is prepared to look through geopolitical risk and focus on core inflation. The 30Y holding steady while 5Y rallies means the market is betting on short-term easing but long-term inflation — a dangerous curve steepener that punishes duration holders. The key question for next week: does the NFP validate the "still hot labor market" narrative that Kashkari needs? If June payrolls come in above 200K, the rate hike tail risk goes from theoretical to real. This is how policy mistakes are made.
